# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** June 22, 2026

Audio Cards ("the app") is developed by Joshua McMahon. This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

## Summary

Audio Cards is **offline-first**. Your flashcards, review history, and settings are stored **only on your device**. We do not operate servers that collect your study data, and we do not use analytics or advertising SDKs.

## Data stored on your device

The app stores the following locally in SQLite and on-device files:

- **Flashcard content** you create or import (front/back text, tags, locales)
- **Imported media** (audio and images from CSV or Anki `.apkg` imports)
- **Scheduling state** (due dates, intervals, learning progress)
- **Review history** (ratings, timestamps, session metadata)
- **App settings** (speech rate, volume, hands-free mode, default languages, study limits)

This data is not transmitted to us or any third-party service we control.

## Data we do not collect

- No user accounts or login
- No email, name, or contact information
- No device identifiers sent to our servers
- No crash reporting or analytics to third parties
- No sale or sharing of your data

## Microphone and speech recognition

If you enable **hands-free mode**, the app uses your device's microphone and speech recognition to listen for voice commands (e.g. "flip", "good", "again") during review.

- Audio is used **only** to recognize short voice commands
- The app does **not** record or store voice audio or transcripts
- Recognition is requested to run **on-device** when supported; on some devices the operating system may use its own speech service (e.g. Apple Speech on iOS)
- You can use the app without hands-free mode and without granting microphone access

## Text-to-speech

Card text is spoken using your device's built-in text-to-speech engine. Text is not sent to our servers.

## Imports

When you import a CSV or Anki `.apkg` file, the file is read from your device and processed locally. Importing does not upload the file to us.

## Data deletion

You can delete individual decks (and all associated cards and history) inside the app. Uninstalling the app removes all local data from your device.

## Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children. The app has no account system.

## Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will change when we do.

## Contact

For privacy questions or support, open an issue on GitHub or use the Support link in the app Settings screen.
